Service brief

Two written artifacts that make failing systems legible.

I work upstream of delivery teams. If your system keeps failing in the same way, I produce the structured document that makes the failure pattern visible and the path to change explicit.

This is not implementation, facilitation, coaching, or program ownership. It is a fixed-scope written intervention designed for internal decision-making and builder handoff.

Diagnosis exposes what is really breaking. Redesign defines what must change, where responsibility moves, and in what order implementation can begin without drift.

01 // Diagnostic Artifact entry engagement

Expose the real structure behind recurring failure.

£10k fixed fee / 14 days
upstream fixed-scope decision-ready document

For systems that keep breaking in the same place under growth, complexity, or organizational scale. This is the point where more meetings have stopped helping and the real structure needs to be made explicit.

What it answers

  • What is this system actually doing, not what it claims to do?
  • Which rules are breaking that must not break?
  • Where do those rules really live?
  • Where does stated design diverge from operating reality?
  • What does that imply builders now need to address?

What you send

The recurring failure pattern, the interfaces involved, and any materials that reflect the current state: diagrams, policies, workflows, notes, contracts, screenshots, or internal documents.

What you receive

One structured diagnostic document for internal decision-making and builder handoff. In practice, it gives decision owners a single source of structural truth and gives builders a clearer basis for what must change first.

Included

  • Clarity on what kind of system you are actually running, and why that matters
  • Invariant definition: the rules that must not break
  • Enforcement visibility: where those rules live in reality, not only in policy
  • Boundary visibility across teams, services, policies, and contracts
  • A build map showing the structural implications for sequence, interfaces, and transition logic

Outside scope

  • Delivery ownership
  • Implementation execution
  • Org-change programs
  • Ongoing coaching or facilitation retainers
02 // Engagement flow how it starts
  • Step 1: Email a Five-Line Brief: The Pattern, The Stake, The Interfaces, Concentration, and Constraints, plus any materials you already have.
  • Step 2: I assess fit and confirm whether the work should begin with Diagnostic or move directly to Redesign.
  • Step 3: If the fit is correct, I return a structured written deliverable designed for internal circulation, decision-making, and builder handoff.

03 // Redesign Artifact follow-on engagement

Define the new boundary and the order of change.

£15k–£25k
post-diagnosis or direct-entry scope-dependent builder handoff

Pricing depends on the breadth of the redesign, the number of interfaces involved, and the complexity of the transition path.

This is the what-changes-now phase. When the failure is already visible, but the route to change is still unclear, this engagement defines what must change, where responsibility moves, and in what order implementation can safely begin.

What it defines

The target boundary, the transition sequence, the interface changes required, the dependency order, and the explicit constraints that prevent local workarounds from quietly reintroducing the same failure pattern.

What you send

Either the completed Diagnostic document plus current materials, or a clear failure description with known constraints if the need for redesign is already obvious and bounded.

What you receive

One structured redesign document that defines the new operating boundary and the order of change. In practice, it gives builders a stable sequence to execute against instead of forcing them to invent design midstream.

Included

  • Boundary redesign
  • Transition sequence and dependency order
  • Interface and handoff clarification
  • Change logic that builders can execute against
  • Explicit constraints that prevent drift, workaround logic, or quiet re-coupling

Outside scope

  • Program management
  • Team delivery leadership
  • Technical build execution
  • Stakeholder alignment workshops
04 // Fit who this is for

This fits if

  • Your system keeps failing in the same way under growth or scale
  • Your teams are stuck in a policy-versus-reality gap
  • Decision owners need structure, not more interpretation
  • Implementation is blocked by upstream ambiguity
  • Builders are being forced to reinvent design during execution

This does not fit if

  • You need implementation support, delivery leadership, or program ownership
  • You are looking for coaching, facilitation, or workshops
  • The problem is too broad or undefined for a bounded written intervention
  • You want open-ended advisory access rather than a fixed-scope artifact
