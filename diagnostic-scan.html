Diagnostic Scan

Read the structure of any failing system.

Four questions. Answer them honestly and the structural problem — if there is one — becomes visible. Use this on any system where the same failure keeps returning under growth.

This is not a quiz. There are no scores. It is a structured method for locating where a system is actually breaking.

01 Step 01 Answer four questions

Purpose, repeated action, rules, failure. Write what is actually happening — not what the system is supposed to do.

02 Step 02 Generate your structural read

The scan identifies where the rule violation is likely sitting and what to examine first before making any changes.

03 Step 03 Email it as your brief

If the pattern points to a structural problem, your four answers are already the five-line brief. Send them directly.

the scan
01 Purpose

What is this system supposed to do? What does it exist to deliver — not what it claims, what it actually does?

02 Repeated action

What is the one action this system performs over and over at its core? The primitive — the smallest unit of real work.

03 Rules

What rules govern that action? What must always be true for the system to work? State the rules as they exist in practice, not in policy.

04 Failure

Where does the system keep breaking? What rule is being violated — and where in the system does that violation actually live?

Answer at least one question to generate a read.
