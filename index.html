What I do

Structural truth for systems that fail under scale.

I write Diagnostic Artifacts that turn recurring failure into explicit structure: primitives, invariants, enforcement, and a build map.

Want one? Email five lines: what keeps breaking, what’s at stake, and the interfaces involved. EMAIL.

What’s not included

01 // Overview what this is

Most “complexity” persists because the system’s real rules are implicit, contested, or enforced in the wrong place. The Artifact makes structure explicit so builders can change the system without drift.

What this replaces

Alignment loops, committee interpretation, “policy says / reality does”, and roadmaps that don’t change failure.

What you can do with it

Hand it to builders. Use it to decide boundaries, enforcement points, and sequence. Reduce debate by making the operating grammar visible.

What improves

Faster decisions, fewer “interpretation gaps”, fewer recurring incidents with new names, safer change under growth.

02 // Method what I map
  • Primitive
    01

    What action the system repeats (the real unit of work).

  • Invariants
    02

    What must never be violated (fail-closed constraints).

  • Enforcement
    03

    Where rules live in reality (not where policy says they live).

  • Mismatch
    04

    Where the interface promise diverges from the operating grammar.

  • Build Map
    05

    The boundary and transition sequence that makes implementation deterministic.
