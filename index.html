02 // Overview What this is

Most systems fail repeatedly because their real rules are implicit, contested, or enforced in the wrong place. Diagnosis makes the structure visible; Redesign specifies what must change, in what order.

Typical triggers

03 // Methods How the work is structured
What makes this different

Written artifacts, not endless meetings. Structural diagnosis, not surface advice.

Diagnostic Method

I make recurring failure structurally explicit:

  • Primitive — the repeated unit of work the system actually performs.
  • Invariants — rules that must not break.
  • Enforcement — where those rules live in operating reality.
  • Mismatch — where stated design and operating reality diverge.
  • Build Map — what must change first, and what depends on what.

Redesign Method

I define the target state and the path to reach it:

  • Target Boundary — what the system should become.
  • Transition Sequence — the order in which change must occur.
  • Interface Changes — what must be tightened, removed, or clarified.
  • Dependency Order — what moves first, and what depends on what.
  • Drift Refusal — gates that prevent local workarounds from re-entering the system.

04 // Seeing Systems method carrier bridge

Seeing Systems teaches structural literacy through pointable carriers rather than abstract theory alone.

Doors, queues, elevators, approval gates, and bottlenecks are not random examples. They are teaching objects. Each one makes a system visible at human scale: purpose, rules, enforcement, load, and failure posture can all be pointed to directly.

  • Door — threshold, permission, fail-open vs fail-closed
  • Queue — fairness, ordering, congestion, bypass pressure
  • Elevator — capacity, sequencing, load under constraint
  • Approval gate — submission, acceptance, rejection, exception paths
  • Bottleneck — where visible flow hides structural concentration

This is also the communication bridge: a way to make complex diagnosis legible to non-specialist stakeholders.

Explore the carrier method in Seeing Systems
05 // Universal Formula canonical architecture
Purpose → Primitive → Medium → OS → Marketplace → Category → Civilization → Meta

The Universal Formula is the scaling architecture behind the broader body of work. It tracks how a primitive becomes an operating system, how that operating system becomes a market structure, and how that market structure can harden into a category or civilization-scale layer.

A diagnostic and construction sequence: purpose, primitive, medium, OS, marketplace, category, civilization, meta. Used to identify which layer is under-designed, wrongly named, or being mistaken for another.

Referenced in The System Founder Framework, it is also part of the larger architecture behind how scale problems are located and named.
