01 // Overview
what I do
Upstream diagnosis for systems that fail under scale.

I produce written Diagnostic Artifacts that make a system’s real structure visible: what it does, what must not break, and where failure concentrates.

Most chronic complexity is not “people problems.” It is topology: misnamed primitives, unclear invariants, and enforcement living in the wrong place.

Deliverable
Diagnostic Artifact (written) — primitives, invariants, enforcement points, failure modes, build map.
Duration
Typically 7–14 days, depending on access and scope.
Inputs
A small set of artifacts: interfaces, policies, incident patterns, and one or two operator interviews.
After
A deterministic basis for specification, architecture, policy refactor, or roadmap sequencing.
Not included

Best fit
Founders, CTOs, operators, and policy leads facing recurring failure under scale.
02 // Method
what I map, in order
  1. Primitive
    01

    What action repeats? Name the irreducible operation the system performs.

  2. Invariants
    02

    What must never break? Define rules as fail-closed, auditable constraints.

  3. Enforcement
    03

    Where do rules actually live? Locate enforcement points and violation handling.

  4. Mismatch
    04

    Where does promise ≠ execution? Identify interface claims vs operating reality.

  5. Build Map
    05

    What order makes it deterministic? Sequence boundaries and transitions for implementation.
