01 // Recognition Does your system look like this?

Buyers don’t need more interpretation. They need the real structure made explicit.

Ghost Rules

Policy says X, reality requires Y to survive. The system runs on exceptions.

Memory Collapse

The same structural failure returns under new names. Decisions don’t persist.

Authority Inversion

People responsible for outcomes cannot change the rules that produce them.

Signal–Reward Mismatch

Metrics improve while real quality declines. The system selects optimisers.

Interface Obscurity

Critical decisions are hidden inside handoffs, tools, and local workarounds.

Boundary Drift

Workarounds quietly re-enter as “exceptions” until failure becomes normal.

02 // What you receive Deliverable composition

Each engagement ends in one written artifact designed for internal circulation, decision-making, and builder handoff.

Diagnostic Artifact includes

  • System topology: where work actually flows
  • Primitive definition: the real unit of work
  • Invariants: rules that must not break
  • Enforcement map: where rules live vs where they break
  • Mismatch analysis: stated design vs operating reality
  • Build map: what must change first, and what depends on what

Redesign Artifact includes

  • Target boundary spec: responsibility/authority/enforcement placement
  • Transition sequence: Step 1 → N
  • Interface tightening: what must be removed, clarified, or gated
  • Dependency order: what moves first, what relies on what
  • Drift refusal gates: constraints that prevent regression

Full scope, delivery terms, and service boundaries live on the Services page.
