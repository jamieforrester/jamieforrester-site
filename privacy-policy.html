04 // Lawful bases uk gdpr basis

Legal basis for processing

Depending on the context, personal data is processed on one or more of the following lawful bases:

Legitimate interests

To run the business responsibly, respond to enquiries, assess fit, manage communications, maintain records, and protect the integrity of the website and services.

Steps before contract / contract performance

Where processing is necessary to evaluate a proposed engagement, prepare documents, or deliver agreed services.

Legal obligation

Where processing is required to meet tax, accounting, regulatory, or other legal obligations.

Consent

If consent is relied on for any specific purpose, that will be made clear at the point of collection. Where consent is the lawful basis, it may be withdrawn at any time.

05 // Sharing and processors who receives data

Who personal data may be shared with

Personal data is not sold. It is shared only where reasonably necessary to run the website and business, or where legally required.

  • Email, hosting, storage, invoicing, or document providers used to operate the business
  • Professional advisers such as accountants, lawyers, or insurers where necessary
  • Authorities or regulators where disclosure is legally required

Processors

Third-party providers used to support the website or business may process data on my behalf under their own terms and security controls. Where such providers are used, they are selected on the basis that they are appropriate for professional business use.

06 // Retention how long data is kept

How long personal data is kept

Personal data is retained only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose it was collected, including record-keeping, legal, tax, and dispute-management needs.

  • General enquiry emails and correspondence: retained for as long as necessary to handle the enquiry and maintain reasonable business records
  • Client and pre-client records: retained for as long as necessary to manage the engagement, related follow-up, and legal/accounting obligations
  • Financial records: retained as required by applicable accounting and tax rules

Retention approach

Where a fixed retention period is not appropriate, records are kept only while there is a legitimate business need, legal requirement, or reasonable need to maintain an accurate history of work and communications.

07 // Security protection measures

How personal data is protected

  • Use of reputable email, hosting, and storage providers
  • Access controls and password protection for business accounts
  • Two-factor authentication where available
  • Data minimisation wherever possible

Client materials and confidentiality

I do not outsource the analysis itself. Client materials are handled only as necessary to assess, scope, administer, or deliver the work, using secured infrastructure appropriate to a sole professional practice.

AI and training use

Client materials and engagement data are not used to train public generative AI tools or shared for model training. They are used only for the purpose of the relevant enquiry or engagement.

No guarantee

No online system can guarantee absolute security. However, reasonable steps are taken to protect personal data against unauthorised access, misuse, loss, or disclosure.
