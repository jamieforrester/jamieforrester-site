Outlier AI

AI task marketplace · private platform
Governing Diagnosis

Outlier recruits through the grammar of an expertise market while operating through the mechanics of a reliability pipeline. The expertise it recruits functions less as a consumed production input than as a legitimacy signal for upstream clients.

Because the intake architecture and the execution architecture select for different inputs, every downstream mechanism — the qualification gate, quality score, routing algorithm, and payment structure — operates on a false premise.

Primitive mismatch: Stated — expert human judgment improves AI systems. Actual — variance-controlled instruction adherence at scale. These have never been the same thing.

NHS Primary Care Continuity System

Public healthcare institution · UK National Health Service
Governing Diagnosis

The NHS has externalised its operational memory function onto the patient by architectural default — while fusing sensing, interpretation, authority, and memory reconstruction into a single ten-minute GP consultation operating without structural support for any of them.

01
01 // Diagnostic Artifact Public

The governing diagnosis, collapse node, and ghost structure of hidden human carriers keeping a £2.8bn-per-year failure operational.

14Sections
FullArtifact
PublicAccess

The NHS has instrumented itself heavily for audit and compliance while the operational continuity function has migrated outward. It generates more data about patients than at any prior point. Less of it arrives, in retrievable form, at the moment a decision must be made.

Primitive mismatch: Promised — joined-up care in which records support clinical decisions across the patient’s whole NHS journey. Actual — each organisation holds its own records, sharing is after-the-fact, and the patient repeats their history as the default mechanism.

What this artifact covers

  • Governing diagnosis and one-line board verdict
  • System boundary map across the GP → referral → specialist → discharge → return lifecycle
  • Structural failure point — memory externalisation at the consultation node
  • Four-function collapse: sensing, interpretation, authority, memory fused
  • Ghost structure — patient, carer, known GP, receptionist as hidden carriers
  • Cost of continuation — £2.8bn negligence annually, 36 PFD warnings, 11 million acute presentations without record access, and repeated digital reform without retrieval-layer correction

What these examples show

Across both systems, the same condition appears: a critical function is absent at the point where consequence is carried.

In both cases, correction remained inside the inherited frame. Redesign required changing the frame itself. Hidden human carriers absorbed what the architecture could not hold — Queue Managers in the platform, patients and carers in the institution. In both cases, the correction loops intensified for years without touching the primitive.

The domain changes. The structure does not. What differs is the system under analysis. What repeats is the failure pattern: a critical function absent at the point where consequence must be carried.
